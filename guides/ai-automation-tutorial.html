Implementation tutorial

Build your first AI support automation.

Move from a repetitive question to a tested, measurable workflow in six steps.

1. Choose one high-volume request

List common questions and select a low-risk topic with a clear answer, such as delivery time, return eligibility, or account setup. Record the current volume, response time, and escalation rate.

2. Prepare approved source content

Collect the current policy, FAQ, product page, or internal guide. Remove contradictions and name an owner who will keep it current.

3. Define boundaries

Write down what the AI may answer, what data it may collect, and which situations must go to a person. Refund exceptions, legal threats, and account-security issues commonly require human review.

4. Test realistic questions

Use direct questions, vague wording, spelling errors, and edge cases. Check factual accuracy, tone, links, and whether uncertain answers escalate safely.

5. Launch gradually

Begin with a limited page or audience. Review conversations daily during the first stage and add missing source content instead of patching individual answers.

6. Measure and improve

Resolution

How many questions finish without human work?

Quality

Are answers accurate, useful, and positively rated?

Risk

Do sensitive cases escalate according to policy?

Launch checklist: owner, current sources, test set, escalation rule, privacy review, and weekly quality review.
