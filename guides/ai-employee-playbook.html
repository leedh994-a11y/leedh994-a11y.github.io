AI employee playbook

Manage AI like a role, not a magic button.

Give an AI worker a defined job, trusted knowledge, limited permissions, and an accountable human owner.

Write a role card

Purpose: answer first-line product and policy questions.

Inputs: approved website pages, guides, and FAQs.

Allowed actions: answer, share links, collect an email, or create a handoff.

Prohibited actions: approve exceptions, change accounts, promise refunds, or expose private data.

Owner: support operations lead.

Build an operating rhythm

Daily

Review escalations, poor ratings, and unanswered questions.

Weekly

Update source content, expand the test set, and inspect trends.

Monthly

Review resolution, quality, savings, risk, and the scope of the role.

Keep people accountable

The AI can execute approved work, but a person remains responsible for policy, source quality, access, review, and customer outcomes. Expand the role only after the current scope performs reliably.

Rule of thumb: if a mistake can create financial, legal, safety, privacy, or trust harm, require explicit human review.
