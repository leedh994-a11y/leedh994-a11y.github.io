AI support introduction

What AI customer support tools actually do.

A practical guide to knowledge-grounded answers, workflow automation, human handoff, and measurement.

Five parts of a useful support system

An AI customer support tool should do more than generate fluent text. It needs trusted source content, a customer-facing channel, rules for actions and escalation, a review loop, and clear measurements.

Knowledge

Website pages, policies, product documents, and approved FAQs ground answers in the business.

Conversation

A website assistant or messaging integration lets customers ask in their own words.

Workflow

Rules decide when to answer, collect an email, create a task, or involve a person.

Analytics

Resolution, satisfaction, missing content, and escalation reveal what to improve.

Questions to ask before choosing a tool

  • Can every answer cite or trace back to approved content?
  • How does the assistant handle uncertainty and sensitive requests?
  • Can it connect to the channels customers already use?
  • Can a human see the history when a conversation is escalated?
  • Who owns updates, testing, privacy, and reporting?
