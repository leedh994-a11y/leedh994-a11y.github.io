The challenge

Repetitive questions consumed the support day.

The store received about 100 questions every day. Most were predictable: delivery time, order tracking, return rules, product details, and stock availability. Agents repeatedly searched the same website pages and policy documents before writing nearly identical replies.

This made fast responses expensive and left less time for damaged orders, high-value buyers, and other conversations requiring judgment.

Support content connected

  • Product and collection pages
  • Shipping and return policies
  • Order-tracking instructions
  • Product care PDFs
  • Human escalation rules

The solution

A trained first-line AI assistant.

1. Train

The team connected its site, policies, and guides to a single knowledge base.

2. Answer

The website assistant handled routine questions instantly, around the clock.

3. Escalate

Complex or sensitive requests were handed to a person with useful context.

The result

More capacity without a larger first-line team.

The AI assistant handled roughly 80 of 100 daily questions. Human agents focused on the remaining 20 conversations, creating an estimated $2,000 in labor savings each month.

Results are an illustrative customer scenario supplied for this case study. Individual results depend on question volume, content quality, and workflow design.
